Our Team

Meet the passionate individuals driving WISE Liberia forward.

Board of Advisors

Kim Ouillette, JD.

Legalazation and Recruitments

Kim Ouillette (she/her) is a Staff Attorney in the Wage Protection Program at Legal Aid at Work, a nonprofit organization that advocates on behalf of low-wage workers in California. Kim is also the co-founder and director of the Liberian Education Fund, a scholarship program for high school and university students in Liberia. She is a graduate of UC Berkeley School of Law and lives in Oakland, California.

Wainwright Acquoi

Innovations and Development

Wainright Acquoi is the CEO of TRIBE – a nonprofit design, strategy, and implementation company that improves learning outcomes, facilitating workforce development and building entrepreneurship ecosystems in Liberia. Wain holds an entrepreneurial degree from the Watson Institute at Lynn University in Florida and previously worked for Dalberg Media in Copenhagen, the African Leadership Group in San Francisco, and Obviyo in Florida.

Alexix Dzirfziech, PHD

Fundraiser and Sustainbility

Alexis Dziedziech is a first generation post-doctoral researcher at the Institut Pasteur, France where she works on the emergence and diagnostics of tick-related bites and disease. She obtained her Ph.D. from Stockholm University, Sweden, and her bachelors from Swarthmore College, USA. She has served on various organizational committees with experience hosting distinguished guests, organizing conferences, and working as a team. With a diverse international education as well as experience teaching, Alexis has personally experienced and witnessed different degrees of difficulties concerning equal access to resources and knowledge transfer. Alexis is dedicated to aiding global and local efforts in advancing student access to basic educational resources.

Prince U.D Tardeh, BSc.

Co-founder and Board Secretariat

Prince is a Global Changemaker and a Lift Liberia Scholar. He graduated from St. Peter Claver Catholic High School in Buchanan, Liberia, and is a current student at Swarthmore College in Pennsylvania, USA. He is an enthusiast of knowledge, leadership, healthcare, and Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Math (STEAM) education. Prince grew up as an advocate for Children’s rights and served as 1st Deputy Speaker for the Liberia National Children’s Representative Forum. He is a writer/storyteller, an emerging poet, and a spoken word artist. He visits the gym, plays football, and listens to music for fun.

Emmanuel M. Nurse, BSc.

Co-founder and Executive Director

Emmanuel holds a Bachelor’s Degree (with honors) in Environmental Science from Stella Maris Polytechnic University. In 2017, he graduated from St. Peter Claver Catholic High School as a Division 2 honors student and a European Union honoree. He’s a professional youth development practitioner with a massive interest in advocacy. Emmanuel is a former Chairperson of the Grand Bassa Children Representative Forum, the Pan African Volunteers Network, and the Network for Peace. In these roles, he advocated for the protection of children’s rights, the eradication of drug abuse, and the promotion of quality education. Currently, he is a Community Development Officer at ArcelorMittal Liberia, where he reviews the Corporate Donations Log, analyzes the Stakeholder Engagement Register, and develops bi-monthly sustainability reports to maintain a sustainable environment. Emmanuel believes “the smallest ideas and potentials undermined today could liberate and impact an entire generation tomorrow.” He finds visiting the gym and singing fun.
